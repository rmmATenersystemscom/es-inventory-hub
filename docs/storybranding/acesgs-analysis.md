#### 4. Glossary / Definitions

Create `/resources/compressed-air-glossary` with 100+ terms:

**Essential Compressed Air Terms:**

- **CFM (Cubic Feet per Minute):** The volume of air flow, measured at specific conditions. The most common measurement for compressor capacity. Example: A 100 CFM compressor delivers 100 cubic feet of air per minute.

- **SCFM (Standard Cubic Feet per Minute):** CFM measured at standard conditions (68°F, 36% relative humidity, 14.7 PSIA at sea level). SCFM allows fair comparison between compressors regardless of altitude or ambient conditions.

- **ACFM (Actual Cubic Feet per Minute):** CFM measured at actual site conditions, accounting for temperature, humidity, and elevation. ACFM is always less than SCFM at higher altitudes or temperatures.

- **PSI (Pounds per Square Inch):** Pressure measurement. PSIG = gauge pressure (above atmospheric). PSIA = absolute pressure (including atmospheric). Most industrial compressed air systems operate at 100-125 PSIG.

- **Duty Cycle:** The percentage of time a compressor runs loaded vs. unloaded. A 75% duty cycle means the compressor produces air 75% of the time. Continuous duty = 100%.

- **Rotary Screw Compressor:** Positive displacement compressor using two interlocking helical rotors to compress air. Ideal for continuous duty, high efficiency, low maintenance. Most common type for industrial applications.

- **Oil-Free Air:** Compressed air containing no oil contamination, either from oil-free compression technology or filtration removing oil aerosols and vapors. Required for food, pharmaceutical, and medical applications.

- **ISO 8573-1:** International air quality standard defining contamination levels for particles, water, and oil in compressed air. Class 0 = technically oil-free. Class 1 = highest achievable with oil-lubricated + filtration.

- **VSD (Variable Speed Drive):** Compressor that varies motor speed to match air demand, rather than running at constant speed. Saves 20-40% energy compared to fixed-speed in variable demand applications.

- **Specific Power:** Energy consumption per unit of air produced, typically measured in kW per 100 CFM. Lower specific power = more efficient. Used to compare compressor efficiency.

- **Air Receiver Tank:** Storage vessel that buffers supply and demand fluctuations, reduces compressor cycling, and provides emergency backup air. Size recommendation: 3-5 gallons per CFM for reciprocating, 1-2 gallons per CFM for rotary screw.

- **Dew Point:** Temperature at which water vapor condenses into liquid. Pressure dew point (PDP) is measured under pressure. Refrigerated dryers achieve +35-40°F PDP. Desiccant dryers achieve -40°F to -100°F PDP.

- **System Pressure Drop:** Difference between compressor discharge pressure and point-of-use pressure due to piping friction, filters, and dryers. Should be <10% (10 PSI on 100 PSI system). Excessive drop wastes energy.
